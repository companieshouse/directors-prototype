Add, update and cease a person with significant control (PSC)

Use this service to update PSC information for limited companies on the Companies House register.

In this service, you can:

  • add a new PSC
  • update an existing PSC's details
  • cease a PSC
  • add or remove a PSC statement
What a PSC statement is
Your PSC register must contain information about all PSCs of the company.
If this is not possible, you must put a statement in your register to explain why this information is not available.

Before you start

You'll need:

  • a Companies House account - we'll ask you to sign in or create one
  • the company number
  • the company authentication code
  • the PSC's natures of control
  • the date they became a PSC, and the date the company's PSC register was updated with this information

What you'll need to add a person as a PSC

You'll need to tell us their:

  • name
  • date of birth
  • nationality
  • correspondence address and home address

You'll also need to tell us whether the PSC has ever applied to protect personal information at Companies House.

What you'll need to add a corporate body as a PSC

You'll need to tell us its:

  • name
  • registered office address
  • corporate body type
  • legal form and governing law
  • register the corporate body is entered in
  • country of registration and registration number

A corporate body can also be known as a relevant legal entity (RLE).

Help with corporate bodies registered outside the UK

If a corporate body is registered outside the UK, it needs to be declared as a PSC if any of these statements apply:

  • its shares are traded on a regulated market in the EU
  • its shares are traded on a specified market in Switzerland, Israel, USA and Japan
  • it is a trust with a single person or corporate body that has more than 50% control over it

What you'll need to add an other registrable person (ORP) as a PSC

You'll need to tell us its:

  • name of office or official position
  • registered office address
  • legal form or corporate identity
  • governing law
What an ORP is

An ORP is typically an organisation, office or official position. For example, it could be a:

  • government or government department of a country or territory
  • local authority or local government body in the UK or elsewhere
  • corporation sole (typically an official position)
